Foxtail Palm

WODYETIA BIFURCATA, the foxtail palm, is a species of palm in the family Arecaceae, native to Queensland, Australia. It is the sole species in the genus Wodyetia. The Palm and Cycads Societies of Australia (PACSOA) describes this palm as follows: "Very attractive palm with long (2-3m.) plumose leaves (hence the name 'Foxtail'), and up to 10m tall with a grey trunk. It produces large (about the size of a duck egg) orange fruit".

Size and Weather Conditions : The Foxtail Palm tree typically grows 20 to 30 feet tall. The canopy spread is around 5 to 10 feet wide. Can handle brief dips tp 30 degree farhenheit. Thrives in hot, humid environments, loves full sun but tolerates partial shade.

Leaves : Pinnate (feather-like), with leaflets arranged along both sides of a central stalk. Each frond can reach 8 to 12 feet long. Leaflets are narrow, pointed, and slightly curved, giving the frond a plume-like, bushy appearance. Color varies from deep green to light green, depending on age and sunlight exposure. Texture is Smooth and glossy, with a soft, flowing movement in the wind.

Flower : Color is Creamy white to pale yellow. Its type is Branched inflorescence (cluster of flowers). The structure Contains both male and female flowers on the same plant (monoecious). Size is Small and not showy, but they produce vibrant fruit afterward.

Fruit : Oval fruits about 2 inches long. Green when young, turning bright orange-red when ripe. Each fruit contains a single seed, used for propagation.
